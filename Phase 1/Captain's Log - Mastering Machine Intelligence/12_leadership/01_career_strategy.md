## 1. The Career Ladder

### 1.1 Individual Contributor (IC) Track
*   **Junior**: "I need help to solve this task."
*   **Senior**: "I can solve this task alone."
*   **Staff**: "I solve problems that traverse multiple teams."
*   **Principal**: "I solve problems the company doesn't know it has yet."

### 1.2 Management Track
*   **EM (Engineering Manager)**: People, Hiring, Happiness.
*   **Director**: Managing Managers, Budget, Alignment.
*   **VP/CTO**: Business Strategy, Board Relations, Vision.

---

## 2. Strategic Frameworks

### 2.1 The AI Maturity Curve
Don't build a Ferrari when you need a bicycle.
1.  **Ad-Hoc**: "Bob runs a script on his laptop."
2.  **Managed**: "We have a shared database."
3.  **Defined**: "We have a CI/CD pipeline."
4.  **Measured**: "We track ROI and Model Drift."
5.  **Optimized**: "AI is innate to the business model."

### 2.2 Buy vs Build
*   **Commodity**: OCR, Speech-to-Text. **BUY** (API).
*   **Core Competency**: Drug Discovery (if you are Pharma). **BUILD** (In-house).

---

## 3. Communication Patterns

### 3.1 Speaking to Executives
*   **Don't say**: "The validation loss decreased by 0.05 via AdamW."
*   **Do say**: "We automated the document processing, saving 20 hours/week, projected to save $50k/year."

### 3.2 Speaking to Engineers
*   **Don't say**: "Just use AI to fix it."
*   **Do say**: "We need a binary classifier with >95% precision. Here is the labeled dataset."

---

## 4. The Exit Games

### 4.1 Startup vs Big Tech
*   **Startup**: High Risk, High Equity. You wear 10 hats. You learn fast.
*   **FAANG**: High Salary, Stability. You are a cog in a giant machine. You learn scale.

### 4.2 Building a Personal Brand
*   Open Source contributions.
*   Technical Blogging (Writing clarifies thinking).
*   Speaking at Conferences (Networking).
*   **"Your network is your net worth."**
